  • There is a small fraction of the flux escapes from the core into the air. The flux outside is called leakage flux.

  • Air gap "fringing effect".

Energy loss in ferromagnetic core

Hysteresis Loss

When a ferromagnetic material is subjected into an alternating MF, BBB always lags behind HHH, which is called Hysteresis.

Root causes:

  1. Friction of domain rotation;
  2. Domain wall pinning (impurities, …\dots…); These effects above cause the process of magnetization to be irreversible. When HHH removed, some domains stay aligned, creating Remanence.

The effect of mmf on the loop

As F=Ni\mathscr{F} = NiF=Ni increasing, the loop grows taller and wider to reach the saturation point. This means hysteresis loss is higher as it equals to the area of the loop.

§ Theory of operation of real single-phase transformers

Secondary side open

  • Secondary side is open circuit.
  • Input voltage and current to measure hysteresis curve.
  • Flux is proportional to vpv_pvp​ and mmf is proportional to ipi_pip​.
  • ip(t)=0i_p(t) = 0ip​(t)=0 for ideal transformer.

Magnetization current in real transformers

  1. The magnetization current imi_mim​ is used to generate mutual flux ϕM\phi_MϕM​.
  2. While secondary side is opened, the current measured at primary side contains two parts and is called excitation current iexi_{ex}iex​, which includes:

  • Magnetization current imi_mim​: To generate mutual flux.
  • Core loss current ih+ei_{h+e}ih+e​: Hysteresis and eddy current.
  • The magnetization current in the transformer is not sinusoidal. The higher-frequency components are due to magnetic saturation. (i↑↑⇒ϕ↑→i \uparrow \uparrow \Rightarrow \phi \uparrow \rightarrowi↑↑⇒ϕ↑→)
  • The fundamental component of imi_mim​ lags the voltage applied to the core by 90∘90^\circ90∘.

Core-loss current

  • The core-loss current ih+ei_{h+e}ih+e​ is nonlinear because of the nonlinear effects of hysteresis.
  • The fundamental component of ih+ei_{h+e}ih+e​ is in phase with the voltage applied to the core.
